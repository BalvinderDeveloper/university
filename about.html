About

Academic Areas

Under the guidance of renowned administrators and faculty members in four departments, students have opportunities
to pursue or advance careers in teaching, educational administration, higher education, research, policymaking, student affairs, rehabilitation counseling, school psychology, kinesiology and athletic training.

Key Priorities

The College of Education has established a reputation for excellence and visionary thinking in its efforts to improve teaching and learning across our nation and world, particularly within the contexts of urban and global education. The faculty remains committed to addressing the educational and physical needs of all learners across the life span, and to working closely with educators, leaders and policymakers in the field.

Why Choose MSU

Innovative teacher education programs, outstanding faculty, relationships with practicing educators and strong research opportunities make Michigan State University a dynamic place for learning to teach and to contribute to the fields of education and kinesiology. The college is proud to claim more than 58,000 alumni, each touching lives in their own way around the world.

Mission & History

Michigan State University has been preparing educators for over 100 years. The College of Education continues to improve education in our global society through a three-part mission: leadership, scholarship and service.
